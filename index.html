Welcome to the homepage of the MedForLab

The group develops the tools, instruments and strategies needed for optimizing the productive functionality of forest systems in relation to the characteristics of the Mediterranean environments applying principles of sustainable management. On one hand, it focuses on the research of the technological components and breeding of fast-growing species with interest in afforestation activities of high economic value. On the other, the group investigates the impacts of global change and silvicultural management on the integrated production of different goods and services using miscellaneous methods and approaches (forest models for producing wood and non-wood supplies, ecophysiology and adaptation genetics) aimed at increasing the resilience of Mediterranean forests. The group also tackles both fundamental and applied physiology relevant for the understanding of tree functioning and management in drought-prone environments. In particular, the group is interested on the interpretation of 1) carbon and water balances and their climate controls, 2) physiological constrains involved in possible evolutionary trade-offs of Mediterranean trees, and 3) long-term responses to climate change and forecasting of adaptive potential useful for defining anticipatory adaptation policies. The group is especially strong in the application of stable isotopes (13C, 18O) to plant ecology, physiology and adaptation, and also in modelling tree responses to management practices and environmental disturbances.
